Understanding Anemia

Learn about anemia and the key blood parameters that help in its detection and diagnosis

What is Anemia?

Anemia is a medical condition characterized by a deficiency in the number of red blood cells or the amount of hemoglobin in the blood. This results in reduced oxygen-carrying capacity of the blood, leading to symptoms such as fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath, and pale skin.

There are several types of anemia, including iron-deficiency anemia, vitamin B12 deficiency anemia, chronic disease anemia, and others. Early detection and proper management are crucial for preventing complications and maintaining optimal health.

Why Early Detection Matters

Early detection of anemia is crucial because it allows for timely intervention and treatment. Untreated anemia can lead to serious complications including heart problems, pregnancy complications, severe fatigue, and weakened immune system.
